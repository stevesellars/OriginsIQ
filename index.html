The problem in this vertical

Shore excursions move fast. Most platforms don't.

Every missed alert, every manual spreadsheet, every last-minute scramble — it's not just friction. It's revenue walking off the gangway.

Schedule changes go unnoticed
Port times shift. Ships reroute. Itineraries change overnight. By the time your team knows, guests are affected and vendors are already scrambling.
Lost time + guest trust
Capacity managed in spreadsheets
Allocation files, tour rosters, bus counts — disconnected sheets, emailed back and forth. One version behind reality. Guests oversold.
Overbookings + penalties
Revenue leaks are invisible
No-shows, underfilled tours, unbilled extras — without real-time visibility, operators never see what they're leaving behind at every port.
Uncaptured revenue daily
Guides & staff out of the loop
Departure times change. Dock locations shift. Guides find out via WhatsApp — if they're lucky. Coordination failures become guest experience failures.
Guest complaints + rework
No single source of truth
Cruise line data in one place. Operator records in another. Bookings in a third. Nobody sees the full picture — so nobody can act fast enough.
Decision lag at every turn
Growth stalls without headcount
More sailings, more ports, more tours — all demand more coordinators. The business can't scale if every new contract means new hires.
Growth ceiling hit early
